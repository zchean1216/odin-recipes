Mac and Cheese

Description

Asam Laksa is a tangy and spicy Malaysian noodle soup that is a true delight for the taste buds. Made with mackerel fish, tamarind, and a variety of aromatic herbs and spices, this dish is a perfect blend of sour, spicy, and savory flavors.

It is typically served with thick rice noodles and garnished with fresh vegetables, mint leaves, and a splash of prawn paste, making it a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g mackerel fish
  • 2 liters water
  • 200g tamarind paste
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 red chilies, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 500g thick rice noodles
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bunch mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp prawn paste (optional)

Steps

  1. Clean and cook the mackerel fish in 2 liters of water until fully cooked. Remove the fish and set aside to cool. Reserve the fish stock.
  2. Once the fish has cooled, debone and flake the fish meat. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot, add the tamarind paste to the reserved fish stock and bring to a boil.
  4. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, red chilies, fish sauce, salt, and sugar to the pot. Let it simmer for about 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Add the flaked fish meat back into the pot and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, cook the thick rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  7. To serve, place a portion of noodles in a bowl and ladle the hot soup over the noodles.
  8. Garnish with julienned cucumber, sliced red onion, mint leaves, and a drizzle of prawn paste if using.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious Asam Laksa!
